Lagos Gardening Tips & Solutions

Expert advice tailored specifically for Lagos's unique climate and urban gardening challenges

Understanding Lagos's Climate

Lagos has a tropical savanna climate with distinct wet and dry seasons. Understanding these seasonal patterns is essential for successful gardening:

April - October

Rainy Season

Characterized by heavy rainfall, high humidity (80-90%), and slightly cooler temperatures (24-28°C). Perfect for growing most vegetables and tropical plants with proper drainage management.

Heavy rainfall Moderate temps

November - March

Dry Season

Marked by little to no rainfall, lower humidity (55-75%), and higher temperatures (25-33°C). Requires consistent watering and sometimes shade for sensitive plants. The harmattan period brings dusty conditions.

High sun exposure Hot temps

Natural Pest Management

Lagos's tropical climate creates perfect conditions for many garden pests. These natural, locally-sourced solutions help manage common pests without harmful chemicals:

Neem Oil Spray

Neem oil is readily available in Lagos markets and works as an excellent broad-spectrum natural pesticide. For a simple spray:

  • Mix 2 teaspoons of neem oil with 1 teaspoon of mild liquid soap in 1 liter of water
  • Shake well and spray on affected plants, focusing on the undersides of leaves
  • Apply early morning or evening, not during hot midday sun
  • Repeat weekly until pests are controlled

Effective against: aphids, whiteflies, mealybugs, and many caterpillars common in Lagos gardens.

Pepper and Garlic Spray

Using locally available hot peppers and garlic creates a potent insect repellent:

  • Blend 3-4 hot peppers (scotch bonnet works well) and 1 bulb of garlic with 2 cups of water
  • Strain the mixture and add to 1 liter of water with 1 tablespoon of liquid soap
  • Let sit overnight, then spray on plants
  • Caution: Wear gloves and avoid contact with eyes when preparing

Effective against: many chewing and sucking insects including grasshoppers that are common during Lagos's dry season.

Companion Planting

Strategic placement of certain plants can deter pests naturally in Lagos gardens:

  • Marigolds: Plant throughout the garden to deter nematodes and many insects
  • Lemongrass: Widely available in Lagos, repels mosquitoes and some garden pests
  • Basil: Plant near tomatoes to improve flavor and repel flies and mosquitoes
  • Scent Leaf (Ocimum gratissimum): This local Nigerian herb repels insects and has medicinal properties

This method works continuously and provides additional herbs and flowers for cooking or decoration.

Beneficial Insects

Encourage these natural predators in your Lagos garden:

  • Ladybugs: Feed on aphids, mealybugs, and scale insects
  • Praying Mantis: Naturally occurring in Lagos, they eat a variety of garden pests
  • Dragonflies: Control mosquitoes and small flying insects
  • Spiders: Though sometimes feared, garden spiders are excellent pest controllers

Attract these beneficial insects by avoiding broad-spectrum pesticides and planting flowers like sunflowers, cosmos, and zinnias that provide habitat and food.
