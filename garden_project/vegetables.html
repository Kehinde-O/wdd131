Growing Vegetables in Lagos

Discover which vegetables thrive in our tropical climate and how to cultivate them successfully

Vegetable Categories

Spinach (Spinacia oleracea)

Fast-growing leafy green that thrives in Lagos's climate, especially during slightly cooler months.

Partial shade Moderate water Easy to grow
Growing Guide

Lettuce (Lactuca sativa)

Several varieties grow well in Lagos with proper care. Best grown in containers during the dry season.

Partial shade Regular water Moderate
Growing Guide

Kale (Brassica oleracea)

Nutrient-dense leafy green that can handle Lagos heat with afternoon shade and sufficient water.

Morning sun Regular water Moderate
Growing Guide

Carrots (Daucus carota)

Grow best in loose, sandy soil. Select shorter varieties for Lagos's climate and container growing.

Full sun Moderate water Moderate
Growing Guide

Sweet Potato (Ipomoea batatas)

Thrives in Lagos's tropical climate. Both tubers and leaves are edible and nutritious.

Full sun Moderate water Easy
Growing Guide

Radish (Raphanus sativus)

Quick-growing root vegetable perfect for beginners. Ready to harvest in just 3-4 weeks.

Partial sun Regular water Very easy
Growing Guide

Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum)

Popular garden vegetable that grows well in Lagos with proper support and pest management.

Full sun Regular water Moderate
Growing Guide

Peppers (Capsicum annuum)

Both sweet and hot peppers thrive in Lagos's heat. Excellent for container gardening.

Full sun Moderate water Moderate
Growing Guide

Okra (Abelmoschus esculentus)

Heat-loving vegetable native to Africa that grows exceptionally well in Lagos's climate.

Full sun Moderate water Easy
Growing Guide

Basil (Ocimum basilicum)

Aromatic herb that grows quickly in Lagos's warm climate. Perfect for containers on balconies.

Partial sun Regular water Easy
Growing Guide

Mint (Mentha)

Vigorous growing herb that thrives in Lagos. Best grown in containers to control its spread.

Partial shade Regular water Very easy
Growing Guide

Rosemary (Salvia rosmarinus)

Drought-tolerant Mediterranean herb that adapts well to Lagos with good drainage and air circulation.

Full sun Low water Moderate
Growing Guide
